About Me

I love puzzles. Some of my favorite puzzles include Sudoku, Killer Sudoku, and Samurai Sudoku. I enjoy jigsaw puzzles and even logic puzzles. My love for puzzles transfers to my career. I love challenges and enjoy problem solving and developing a solution or more if the problem calls for it.

A student who was nonverbal with only light perception and numerous behavior challenges could not find his way to the restroom even with his desk next to the door. How will he find his way when he doesn’t use a cane? How will he find his way independently? A lightbulb came on! I taped a rope around his desk to the wall and up to the door knob so he could trail the rope and find his way. That solution was simple but one I was very proud of.

I teach students from a variety of levels who are blind or have a visual impairment. I collaborate with the educational team for each student to either develop a course of action or to support the team members to work with the students. I hear problems from my fellow colleagues and have wanted to solve a new problem which led me to work towards becoming a web developer. I am very interested in web accessibility for everyone.

I apply my love for puzzles to help me grow and develop in the field of web development.

Feel free to reach out if you’d like to connect.
